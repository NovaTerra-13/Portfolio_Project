My Master Plan (time management)

Being in the workforce for decades has given me plenty of opportunites to hone my time management skills, especially having worked across corporate and start up type environments. When I had my twins, I honed them even more. It also helps that one of the critical components of my character is accountability.

These are the general best practices I use for time management:

  1. Everything is on a schedule and every schedule syncs
    • So I don't have to track across multiple calendars.
  2. Preparation time
    • I block out half an hour at the beginning and the end of each day to focus on what's coming.
  3. Tackle big things first
  4. Chunk it out
    • Break things into smaller chunks of work. Great for building momentum and staying motivated.
  5. Build flexibility in
    • "The best laid plans often blow up" was some great advice given to me young in my career. Allowing for flexibility is key for navigating inevitable interruptions.
  6. "Don't worry about it today, think about it tomorrow."
    • This advice was given by my spouse's nana and I used to think it was encouraging procrastination. However, I've come to realize it's about realizing that sometimes we need to pause, whether that's for a break or to approach the problem with fresh eyes and mind the next day.
